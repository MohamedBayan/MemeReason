You are an expert annotator for a fine-grained Arabic propaganda classification task.
I will provide you with an image (which may or may not be a meme) and the text extracted from it.

Defining Propaganda:
Propaganda is any communication that deliberately misrepresents symbols and/or entities, appealing to emotions and prejudices while bypassing rational thought, to influence its audience toward a specific goal.

Your task has three steps:

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Identify the propaganda technique(s) used in the meme.

Use the following standardized technique list. Each technique includes a short definition to guide your annotation. Select all techniques that apply. If none apply, return ["none"].

PROPAGANDA TECHNIQUE DEFINITIONS:

1. name_calling  
   Assigning insulting or emotionally charged labels to a person or group.

2. reductio_ad_hitlerum  
   Comparing the target to Nazis, Hitler, or other extremely negative symbols.

3. casting_doubt  
   Undermining credibility rather than addressing the argument.

4. appeal_to_hypocrisy  
   Responding to criticism by pointing out inconsistency instead of addressing the claim.

5. smears  
   Attacking the moral character of a person/group with strongly negative claims.

6. flag_waving  
   Appealing to national pride, patriotism, or group loyalty to justify an idea.

7. appeal_to_authority  
   Using the opinion of an authority figure as proof without proper reasoning.

8. bandwagon  
   Suggesting an idea is valid because “everyone” supports it.

9. appeal_to_values  
   Linking the message to shared values (tradition, religion, morality).

10. appeal_to_fear  
   Using fear, threat, or prejudice to influence the audience.

11. straw_man  
   Misrepresenting the opponent’s argument to make it easier to attack.

12. red_herring  
   Introducing irrelevant information to distract from the main issue.

13. whataboutism  
   Redirecting discussion to a different issue instead of responding.

14. causal_oversimplification  
   Reducing a complex situation to a single cause.

15. black_and_white_fallacy  
   Presenting only two options when more exist.

16. slippery_slope  
   Claiming one small action will inevitably lead to extreme consequences.

17. slogans  
   Using short, emotionally charged, memorable phrases.

18. thought_terminating_cliche  
   Using clichés or fixed phrases to shut down debate (“It is what it is,” etc.).

19. appeal_to_time_pressure  
   Creating false urgency (“act now before it’s too late”).

20. loaded_language  
   Using emotionally charged words to provoke strong feelings.

21. obfuscation  
   Using vague, confusing, or ambiguous language to hide meaning.

22. exaggeration_minimisation  
   Overstating or understating events beyond reality.

23. repetition  
   Repeating the same message to increase impact or memorability.

24. other  
   Any persuasive technique not listed above.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Determine the FINAL 4-class label.

You must assign exactly ONE label from:
- not-meme
- other
- not-propaganda
- propaganda

Label definitions (4 classes):
1) not-meme
   - The image does not follow the definition of a meme.

2) other

     1. Memes that rely on nudity and offensive content, unless the target of the offense is a famous, political, or religious entity.
     2. Memes that rely on numbers or figures to construct meaning.
     3. Memes that show explicit nudity.
     4. Memes that explicitly use offensive words.
     5. Memes that are in a different language (not Arabic).
     6. Memes that you could not understand due to the dialect it was written in, poor font size, or for any other reason.
   - Note: Memes might contain words that have an implicitly offensive meaning, or offensive words may be aimed at social, religious, or political groups. In these cases, do NOT automatically assign "other"; proceed to decide whether it is propaganda.

3) not-propaganda
   - The image is a meme and does NOT contain propaganda techniques.

4) propaganda
   - The image is a meme and DOES contain propaganda techniques (from the list above) to influence attitudes toward a person/group/entity/idea.

Decision order (important):
1) If it is not a meme -> not-meme
2) Else if it is a meme and meets any "other" criterion -> other
3) Else -> decide between propaganda vs not-propaganda

Consistency rule between techniques and final label:
- If final label is propaganda: Techniques must contain at least one technique (not ["none"]).
- If final label is not-propaganda OR not-meme OR other: Techniques must be ["none"] (and do not add per-technique rationales).
