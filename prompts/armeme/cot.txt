Annotation Guideline:

What is a Meme:
A meme is an image with added text that creates humor, commentary, or a clear message. It is meant to be quick to understand and easy to share.

Key Points:
1. Has text placed on the image.
2. The text is humorous, sarcastic, or gives commentary.
3. The meaning comes from the combination of image and text.
4. The image may be edited.
5. Text can be placed anywhere.
6. May use people, animals, or characters with expressions that add meaning.
7. May show actions that help create the message.
8. May use cultural or symbolic images.
9. May use screenshots from movies or shows with added comments.
10. The same image can be reused with new text.

---

What is Propaganda:
Propaganda is any communication that deliberately misrepresents symbols and/or entities, appealing to emotions and prejudices while bypassing rational thought, to influence its audience toward a specific goal.

Labels (4 classes):
1. Not-Meme (label: not-meme)
   - The image does not follow the definition of a meme.

2. Other (label: other)
   - The image can be defined as a meme, but falls under ANY of the criteria below:
     1) Memes that rely on nudity and offensive content, unless the target of the offense is a famous, political, or religious entity.
     2) Memes that rely on numbers or figures to construct meaning.
     3) Memes that show explicit nudity.
     4) Memes that explicitly use offensive words.
     5) Memes that are in a different language (not Arabic).
     6) Memes that you could not understand due to the dialect it was written in, poor font size, or for any other reason.
   - Note: Memes might contain words that have an implicitly offensive meaning, or offensive words aimed at social, religious, or political groups. In these cases, do NOT automatically assign "other"; proceed to decide whether it is propaganda.

3. Not Propaganda (label: not-propaganda)
   - The image is a meme and does not contain propaganda techniques.

4. Propaganda (label: propaganda)
   - The image is a meme and contains propaganda techniques (e.g., emotional manipulation, biased framing, demonization, scapegoating, incitement, misinformation) aimed at influencing attitudes toward a person, group, entity, or idea.

Decision order (important):
1) If it is not a meme -> not-meme
2) Else if it is a meme and meets any "Other" criterion -> other
3) Else -> decide between propaganda vs not-propaganda
