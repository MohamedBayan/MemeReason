PROPAGANDA TECHNIQUE DEFINITIONS (23 TECHNIQUES)

1. NAME CALLING / LABELING
Assigning insulting or emotionally charged labels to a person/group.
Example: “terrorist”, “cattle”, “herd”.

2. REDUCTIO AD HITLERUM
Linking the target to Nazis, Hitler, or other strongly negative groups.
Example: “Their actions remind us of the Nazi era.”

3. CASTING DOUBT
Undermining credibility rather than addressing arguments.
Example: “Raises questions about their effectiveness.”

4. APPEAL TO HYPOCRISY (“YOU ALSO DO IT”)
Accusing the opponent of inconsistency instead of addressing argument.

5. SMEARS (CHARACTER ASSASSINATION)
Attacking moral character with strongly negative claims.

6. FLAG-WAVING
Justifying actions by appealing to national pride or group loyalty.

7. APPEAL TO AUTHORITY
Using authority opinions as proof without proper reasoning.

8. BANDWAGON
Claiming an idea is correct because “everyone agrees”.

9. APPEAL TO VALUES
Linking ideas to positive shared values (tradition, religion, democracy, morality).

10. APPEAL TO FEAR / PREJUDICE
Using fear or stereotypes to push acceptance of an idea.

11. STRAW MAN
Misrepresenting an opponent’s argument into a weaker version.

12. RED HERRING
Introducing irrelevant information to distract from the main issue.

13. WHATABOUTISM
Responding by diverting attention to another issue (e.g., “what about X?”).

14. CAUSAL OVERSIMPLIFICATION
Claiming a complex issue has only one cause.

15. BLACK-AND-WHITE FALLACY (FALSE DILEMMA)
Presenting only two options when more exist.

16. SLIPPERY SLOPE (CONSEQUENTIAL OVERSIMPLIFICATION)
Claiming one action will lead to extreme consequences.

17. SLOGANS
Short, catchy, emotionally loaded phrases.

18. THOUGHT-TERMINATING CLICHÉ
Phrases that shut down debate or critical thinking.
Example: “It is what it is.”

19. APPEAL TO TIME PRESSURE
Forcing urgency: “act now”, “before it's too late”.

20. LOADED LANGUAGE
Emotionally charged words meant to provoke strong feelings.

21. OBFUSCATION / VAGUENESS / CONFUSION
Using unclear or ambiguous language to obscure meaning.

22. EXAGGERATION OR MINIMISATION
Overstating or downplaying events beyond reality.

23. REPETITION
Repeating the same message or phrase for emphasis.

24. OTHER
Any persuasive strategy not covered above.
