Annotation Guideline:

What is a Meme:
A meme is an image with overlaid text designed to convey humor, commentary, or a social message.
The meaning usually emerges from the interaction between the image and the text.

Key points:
1. Contains both image and added text.
2. The text gives context, sarcasm, or commentary.
3. The meaning is derived from the image–text combination.
4. Can use humor, exaggeration, or stereotypes to convey a message.
5. Can be edited, cropped, or remixed.
6. May use popular cultural or social figures.

---

What is Hate Speech:
In this task, hate speech refers to direct or indirect attacks on people based on protected characteristics, including:
- Ethnicity
- Race
- Nationality or immigration status
- Religion or caste
- Sex or gender identity
- Sexual orientation
- Disability or disease

An attack can include:
1. Dehumanizing language (e.g., comparing people to animals or objects).
2. Statements of inferiority or exclusion (e.g., implying a group is less intelligent or should be removed).
3. Mocking or trivializing hate crimes.
4. Violent or hostile intent.

Exceptions:
- Criticizing an individual (not based on a protected trait) is not hate.
- Criticizing organizations or violent groups (e.g., terrorist groups) is not hate.

Categories:
1. not-hateful

   - The image–text combination contributes to an attack or insult.
   - The hateful intent might be implicit (sarcasm, visual metaphor, stereotype) or explicit (slurs, violence, mockery).
