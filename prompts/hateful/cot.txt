Annotation Guideline

What is a Meme:
A meme is an image with overlaid text designed to convey humor, commentary, or a social message.
The meaning usually emerges from the interaction between the image and the text.

Key Points:
1. Contains both image and added text.
2. The text gives context, sarcasm, or commentary.
3. Meaning emerges from the image–text combination.
4. May use humor, exaggeration, or stereotypes.
5. May involve edits, crops, or cultural figures.

---

What is Hate Speech:
Hate speech refers to direct or indirect attacks on people based on protected characteristics, including:
- Ethnicity, race, nationality, or immigration status
- Religion or caste
- Sex or gender identity
- Sexual orientation
- Disability or disease

An attack can include:
1. Dehumanizing comparisons (e.g., animals, objects).
2. Statements of inferiority or exclusion.
3. Mocking or trivializing hate crimes.
4. Violent or hostile intent.

Exceptions:
- Criticizing individuals not based on a protected trait = Not hate.
- Criticizing organizations or violent groups = Not hate.

---

Categories:
1. not-hateful
   - Contains image and text, but no hate speech as per the definition.
   - May include jokes or commentary without targeting protected groups.

2. hateful
   - The meme conveys a hateful or demeaning message toward a protected group.
   - The image–text combination contributes to an insult or attack.
   - Intent can be implicit (sarcasm, stereotype) or explicit (slurs, mockery).
